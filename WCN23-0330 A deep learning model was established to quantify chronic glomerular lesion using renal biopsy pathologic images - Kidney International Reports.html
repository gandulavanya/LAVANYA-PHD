    Introduction

    Assessing the degree of glomerulopathy is an important step in the process of renal pathological diagnosis, which determines the prognosis and response to treatment of patients. This study aimed to build a robust glomerular segmentation and classification model to quantify chronic lesion.

    Methods

    The periodic acidschiff staining using renal fine needle aspiration biopsy sections from Zhongda Hospital renal pathology database were used to build a dataset for training and testing. There were 3205 glomeruli from 176 patients, including 432 sclerotic glomeruli (SG), 150 fibrous crescents (FC), 181 cellular crescents (CC), 117 incomplete glomeruli (glomerular vascular clusters<50%, IG), and 2325 other glomeruli excluding the above four categories (OG). A publicly available renal pathology dataset, Academia and Industry Collaboration for Digital Pathology (AIDPATH), was included as an external validation set, comprising 208 SC, 60 FC, 40 CC, 39 IG, and 498 OG. The neural network adopted Swin-Transform module as the encoder and VGG module as the decoder, with the same jump connection between the encoder and decoder as in U-Net++. Due to the imbalance in classes, a dichotomous classification of OG versus other categories was performed before a quadruple classification.

    Results

    In the dataset of Zhongda Hospital, the accuracy of binary classification of OG and other categories is 0.9226, and the accuracy of AIDPATH group is equivalent (0.8973). GS, FC, CC, IG were respectively classified with the accuracy of 0.9491, 0.9546, 0.9305 and 0.9532, while 0.8328, 0.8985, 0.9045 and 0.8746 respectively in external validation set.

    Conclusions

    These findings demonstrated that the model is efficient and robust in segmentation and classification of PAS-stained glomeruli. Both datasets achieved satisfactory instance segmentation efficacy, which suggests that the model can be generalized and used as an auxiliary tool for pathologists to improve the efficiency of clinical diagnosis.
